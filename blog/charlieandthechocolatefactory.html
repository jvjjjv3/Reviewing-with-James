The Irresistible Charm of Charlie and the Chocolate Factory | 12/1/23

                         

Charlie and the Chocolate Factory is a popular children’s book that was authored by Roald Dahl in 1964. The novel features Charlie Bucket, who was a boy who came from a poor family. He lived with his parents, Mr. and Mrs. Bucket, and his four grandparents, Grandpa Joe, Grandma Josephine, Grandpa George, and Grandma Georgina. The family was very close and would always spend time together, often laughing and joking.

                                                   

One day, Charlie’s family were reading a newspaper and found out that Willy Wonka, who is the owner of a famous chocolate factory and chocolate brand, is deciding to let a select amount of people to tour his factory. His brand of chocolate is called Wonka, and he decided to put five golden tickets inside random chocolate bars and distribute them worldwide. If such a person were to find a golden ticket, that means that they were selected. On top of that. Willy Wonka promised that everyone who comes to the tour will be given enough chocolates to last the rest of their lives!

                         

Although poor, Charlie managed to find some money on the floor, and together with enough luck, he struck himself a golden ticket. Alongside Charlie who was the last of the five to find the tickets, other winners included: Augustus Gloop, Veruca Salt, Violet Beauregarde, and Mike Teavee. When Charlie ran home to show his family that he won, at first they didn’t believe him. But after carefully examining the ticket, it was determined to be authentic, which caused them great joy. In addition, the ticket said to meet at the gates of Willy Wonka’s factory at 10AM sharp on the first day of February, and that they were allowed to bring one or two members of their family with them.

            

When that day came, all the winners of the tickets gathered at the gates of the factory. When it was time, Willy Wonka came out to greet the winners and invited them and their families inside. When they walked inside, they saw various different colors all around them, and they smelt nothing but the sweet aroma of chocolate. During the tour, they saw a chocolate river, an Inventing Room, Oompa Loompas(small human-like creatures), a Nut Room, and a Television Chocolate Room producing chocolates seemingly out of the air. Everyone loved the tour, but unfortunately most people got distracted with all the different desserts and whatnot that they lost track of the group! Except for Charlie and his family, of course, they made it all the way to the end of the tour.

            

I have a lot of respect for this book and for the author. Roald Dahl’s writing appeals to people of all different ages. This book has and continues to bring me great happiness and I hope it is the same for you. If you are interested in reading this wonderful story, I suggest either going to your local bookstore to pick it up or to order it off Amazon. You won’t be disappointed!
